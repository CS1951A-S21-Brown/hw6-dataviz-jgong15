Written Questions

1) Answering the questions

- Graph 1 answers Question 1 by showing the top 10 video games in a selected year (using the select element), ordered by global sales.

- Graph 2 answers the Question 2 by showing the relative shares of the top 10 genres in a selected region (using the select element), with a tooltip on hover displaying genre and gross sales.

- Graph 3 answers Question 3 by showing the top 10 publishers in a selected genre (using the select element), ordered by global sales.

2) Role of D3

- One role of D3 is the possiblity of being able to display data in a webpage format, rather than in a separate application such as word or a PNG.

- Another role of D3 is the the ability to customize the type of information displayed, especially in comparison to an alternative like excel.

- A third role of D3 is the ability to inserte interactive elements into data, such as select systems and tooltips on hover.

3) Why not D3?

One time D3 is not the best is when visualizing data in a non-web context, such as when making a powerpoint presentation.

Another time D3 is not the best is when wanting to make a quick chart, and a tool like Plotly may be better.

A third time D3 is not the best is when you need greater control, as a tool like Matplotlib may be more capable.

4) Accessibility

One accesibility issue is that in my second graph (the Pie Chart) one cannot see the information points without hovering over the sections of the pie (as is advised against in the gif of Amanda Miller's article). This can be cured by adding labels to the pie chart indicating the related genre (unfortuately technical difficulties prevented this from working).

A second accessiblity issue is that my graphs may be difficult to read for the colorblind, especially for those with tritanopia in the bar graphs.

I could have spent more time making sure that the colors of my bar graphs and pie graph were unique and more accessible to the colorblind. What prevented me from doing this was primarily time limitations. This is, however, partially mitigated by the spaces between different parts of graphs.
